Chemical Composition of Wildfires

Wildfire smoke is a complex mixture of gaseous pollutants, hazardous air pollutants, water vapor, and particulate matter.

Particulate matter, also known as PM, is a term used to describe particles composed of solid and liquid matter that are suspended in the air. Wildfires are a common source of particulate matter. The exact composition of PM varies depending on the source.

Seven Chemical Components of PM2.5:
